
The LaCrosse 2300 series has a nasty bug where it seems to read 114mph when it 
(too frequently) loses wireless synch with the outside sensors.  This causes
many errors in historical hi/low, noaa reports, etc.

To find these records:

echo "SELECT datetime(maxtime,'unixepoch','localtime'),dateTime,windSpeed FROM archive WHERE (windSpeed > 113);" | sqlite3 mydb.sdb
echo "SELECT datetime(maxtime,'unixepoch','localtime'),dateTime,windSpeed FROM archive WHERE (windGust  > 112);" | sqlite3 mydb.sdb

echo "SELECT datetime(maxtime,'unixepoch','localtime'),dateTime,max FROM archive_windSpeed WHERE (max  > 112);" | sqlite3 mydb.sdb
echo "SELECT datetime(maxtime,'unixepoch','localtime'),dateTime,max FROM archive_windGust  WHERE (max  > 112);" | sqlite3 mydb.sdb

To null out these fields:

echo "UPDATE archive SET windSpeed=NULL WHERE (windSpeed > 112);" | sqlite3 mydb.sdb
echo "UPDATE archive SET windGust=NULL  WHERE (windGust  > 112);" | sqlite3 mydb.sdb

Then:

weectl database drop-daily
weectl database rebuild-daily

or just do a specified date or range of dates...

weectl database rebuild-daily
            [[--date=YYYY-mm-dd] | [--from=YYYY-mm-dd] [--to=YYYY-mm-dd]]
            [--config=FILENAME] [--binding=BINDING-NAME]
            [--dry-run] [-y]

==> as always, work off a temporary copy of your db just in case
